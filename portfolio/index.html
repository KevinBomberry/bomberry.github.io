Project Auguste for Adobe

Project Auguste, a touch-first interactive sculpting mobile app, is part of Adobe's Project Felix. My task was to take a technology and wrap a product design around it that would comply with and extend Adobe's application aestetic. It took three months and a demo was presented during Adobe MAX 2016.

Project Auguste's engine was designed by Adobe's Sebastian Marketsmueller and allows the user to "sculpt" in a virtual space, complete with various brushes, filters, and symmetrical drawing effects. I introduced an interaction system that would allow for users to interact with the "camera" object while at the same time editing the object's surface using a combination of multi-touch gestures and optionally using a stylus.

The video above shows Adobe's Daichi Ito using Project Auguste demo app on Surface Book.

Adobe Photoshop (XD Lead for 3D)

For five years I worked on Adobe Photoshop’s 3D toolset, moving it from the modal Repoussé dialog in Photoshop CS3, into an intergraded experience and “first-class” citizen of Adobe Photoshop CC.

About Me

I started in theater and music, later moving into restaurants then management, only to find design and in my early 20’s. That was it. Design was everywhere and everything. I knew then I wanted to be a part of it.

I’ve been focusing on various systems of design for the past 25 years, eventually leading me to focus in the realm of user interaction models and experiences for the multi-input-output device and systems.

Very soon more than 75 million active iPhone users will have access to touch-based AR functionality — and the interest in the experience and positional is massive. There will be a more pervasive use of AR, leading to the onset of wearable AR devices, such as Google’s Glass, and VR will be right behind.

But how do we interact in a wearable world in a familiar way that translates across input devices and systems.

My current goal is to understand the complexity of having multiple input systems, bring cohesion to the form and function of the user experience. At the same time, helping move the user to their desired task as efficiently as possible.

Some of my current interest include; concepts of contextual interaction systems and how they relate to individuals and groups in a realtime immersive or partially-immersive autonomous or collaborative environment… and cooking.

Current Work

I have been is a deep-dive mindset for the past few months pushing through 3D and into the user experience and interaction realm for various input devices, like; keyboards, mice, trackpads, touch devices, vision/depth capture, audio, and various game/VR controllers and devices.

There needs to be a clear path through all devices and context that allows the user to easily interact with the content — it cannot be a chore for the user on the 3rd time or you might have just lost them forever. Bye.

So I’m working on some presentations that will talk about the user experience for the upcoming AR onset and next wearable transition, and how to create a more familiar but adaptable user experience.
